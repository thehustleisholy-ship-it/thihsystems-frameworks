# Safety Boundaries

- Not diagnosis, triage, treatment, or a substitute for emergency or clinical care.
- Never tell a user that the absence of a listed sign means they are safe.
- Never delay emergency care to complete documentation.
- Never rank a person’s credibility or infer deception.
- Show CDC source dates and the one-year education window.
- Separate user statements, advocate statements, clinical records, allegations, and adjudicated findings.
- Collect the least data necessary; prohibit advertising trackers in authenticated or symptom-entry surfaces.
- Require threat modeling, retention/deletion rules, access logs, encryption, incident response, and jurisdictional privacy review before real data.
- Require obstetric and emergency escalation review before testing with users.
