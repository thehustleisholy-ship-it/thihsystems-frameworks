# Interactive Specification

Status: concept specification; no validated clinical tool exists.

## In scope for a future prototype

- Patient-entered symptom documentation with timestamps and edit history.
- Generic, source-linked CDC Hear Her warning-sign education.
- Patient or advocate logging of requests for help and responses received.
- A user-initiated emergency-call link that requires an explicit action and does not decide whether an emergency exists.
- Export of a human-readable chronology with provenance metadata.

## Out of scope without new review

- Patient-specific risk scoring, clinical interpretation, diagnosis, triage, treatment direction, medication advice, or a promise that stored material will be admissible evidence.

## Boundary triggers

Patient-specific interpretation or risk prioritization may raise FDA device questions. Hospital provision or operation may create HIPAA/business-associate duties. Direct-to-consumer operation may implicate FTC and state privacy/breach rules. Recording, hashing, audit logs, edits, exports, and custody transfers affect evidentiary integrity but do not guarantee admissibility. Any clinical routing or response promise raises clinical-liability and operational-duty questions.

## Required visible states

Generic education; user-entered statement; advocate-entered statement; emergency link initiated; escalation requested; response logged; record corrected; export generated; source unavailable; and “not clinical advice.” Synthetic scenarios must be labeled synthetic.
