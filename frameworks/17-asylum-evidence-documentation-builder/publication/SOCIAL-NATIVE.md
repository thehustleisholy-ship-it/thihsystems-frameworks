## LinkedIn — personal profile

I keep returning to one distinction: truth and record completeness are not the same thing. Crisis can scatter records across devices, languages, countries, and people; trauma can affect recall for some people, although the research varies [C3]. The hidden failure is that software rewards a clean chronology and may pressure an unknown into a guess. Framework 17 proposes a bounded organizer for user-confirmed chronology, provenance, translation queues, unavailable-evidence explanations, and questions for qualified counsel [C1]. AI could sort and flag; it must never invent facts, decide credibility or eligibility, coach testimony, predict approval, advise on a case, or file. ## Instagram — caption

When the truth is real but the record is incomplete, the problem is not automatically the person. Crisis can scatter dates, messages, identity records, translations, and witness contacts. Some trauma and mental-health research reports disrupted or less-specific recall for portions of refugee populations, with important limits [C3]. Yet digital tools often reward a single clean answer. Framework 17 proposes something narrower: preserve exact, approximate, unknown, and conflicted information; inventory sources; queue translations; record why evidence is unavailable; and prepare questions for qualified counsel [C1]. AI may organize confirmed information. It must not fill gaps, erase inconsistency, assess credibility, determine eligibility, coach testimony, predict outcomes, advise, or file.
