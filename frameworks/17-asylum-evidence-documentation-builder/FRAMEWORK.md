# Framework specification

## Public thesis

Truth and record completeness are not the same thing. A bounded preparation layer could help a person organize sources and uncertainty before speaking with qualified counsel without turning software into an adjudicator, witness coach, or legal service.

## Core burden

Chronology may be affected by displacement, trauma, approximate dates, alternate calendars, translation, transliteration, inaccessible accounts, document loss, and safety risks to witnesses or relatives. The framework treats each of those as information to preserve—not noise to smooth away.

## Data and pattern engine

The schemas keep chronology, evidence items, translation tasks, conflicts, legal-review questions, and export consent separate. The deterministic engine counts visible categories and missing fields. It does not read narrative semantics or map facts to asylum law.

## Export format

A hypothetical export contains an inventory, uncertainty annotations, translation queue, missing-evidence explanations written by the person, and questions for counsel. Originals remain unchanged and linked by provenance. Export is disabled until recipient, minimization, consent, and safety checks are recorded.

## Human, legal, and AI boundaries

Only the person confirms facts. Qualified practitioners interpret law. Human interpreters/translators preserve meaning and meet applicable certification rules. AI may organize user-confirmed metadata; it may not invent, assess, coach, advise, decide, or submit.

## Builder invitation

Continuation should be corrected and governed by people with lived experience alongside qualified immigration, interpretation, accessibility, trauma, privacy, and security practitioners.
