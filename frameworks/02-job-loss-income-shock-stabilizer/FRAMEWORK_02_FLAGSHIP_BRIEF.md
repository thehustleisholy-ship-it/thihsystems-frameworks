# FRAMEWORK 02: FLAGSHIP BRIEF

**Job-Loss & Income-Shock Stabilizer**

A 30-day intensive coordination framework linking unemployment insurance, workforce development, and emergency assistance to prevent cascading financial instability and accelerate reemployment.

---

## Hidden Foundation: Why Job Loss Matters

**The Problem:**
Job loss is not just income loss. It's a compound crisis: income drops, housing becomes unstable, childcare arrangements break, credit deteriorates, healthcare access falters, family stress peaks. Most U.S. households lack emergency savings ($400+ emergency would require debt or crisis support). The first 30 days—between job loss and first crisis decision—determine the next 3 years of financial trajectory.

**Current System Failure:**
Three separate government systems (unemployment insurance, workforce development, emergency assistance) react independently rather than coordinate. A displaced worker navigates three bureaucracies while in acute distress, making reactive decisions that compound harm.

**Evidence:**
- Job displacement causes 20-40% permanent earnings loss (Jacobson, LaLonde, Sullivan)
- 40% of U.S. adults couldn't cover $400 emergency (Federal Reserve SHED)
- First month of job loss determines earnings recovery curve for 3 years (BLS research)
- Without intervention, ~50% of job-loss claimants never return to pre-loss wage level

## Framework 02: The 30-Day Architecture

### What This Maps

A **coordinated response system** that activates within 48 hours of job loss and provides intensive support through 30 days of:

1. **Immediate Financial Assessment** (Days 1-3)
   - Financial situation snapshot (income, expenses, debt, savings)
   - Benefits eligibility screening (UI, SNAP, childcare, housing, LIHEAP)
   - Career situation assessment (skills, target roles, flexibility)

2. **Benefits Cliff Navigation** (Days 4-7)
   - Personalized benefits calculation for different reemployment scenarios
   - Identification of "danger zones" where earning income triggers benefit cliffs
   - Reemployment strategy accounting for tax/benefit phase-outs
   - Financial triage (which bills to pay first? Where to access emergency assistance?)

3. **Job Search Acceleration** (Days 8-21)
   - Job market overview & opportunity mapping
   - Resume optimization & LinkedIn profile setup
   - Interview coaching & mock interview practice
   - Employer target list (10-15 specific companies identified)
   - Weekly check-ins tracking progress

4. **Offer Evaluation & Decision Support** (Days 22-30)
   - When offers arrive: Evaluate fit, wage, benefits, cliffs
   - Negotiation guidance (wage, benefits, timing)
   - Informed acceptance decision aligned with financial plan
   - Extended support options (if not yet employed)

### Why 30 Days

**Research:** The 30-day window is when:
- Initial panic/shock phase ends (decisions become more intentional)
- First UI payment arrives (temporary financial relief window)
- Job search begins bearing fruit (interviews, offers may appear)
- Emergency decisions happen (housing stability, debt) that set trajectory

Intervention after day 30 is too late; financial damage is done. Intervention before day 1 is impossible (person doesn't know they've lost job yet).

## Affected Public

**Primary:** Displaced workers (job loss due to layoff, business closure, industry disruption)
- ~2 million/year in U.S. (BLS displaced worker survey)
- Concentrated in manufacturing, retail, healthcare, transportation
- Disproportionately affect older workers (45+), workers of color, workers without bachelor's degree

**Secondary:** 
- Families dependent on displaced worker income
- Landlords (housing instability if rents unpaid)
- Employers (hiring during reemployment phase)
- Communities (economic impact of unemployment)

**Tertiary:**
- Childcare providers (families may lose subsidy or miss payments)
- Credit creditors (housing, auto, credit card debt affects recovery)
- Nonprofit emergency assistance (fewer calls for help if prevented early)

## Core Data Inputs Required

**To Operate:**
- Participant financial situation (income, expenses, debt, savings)
- Employment history & skills profile
- Benefits eligibility data (UI, SNAP, childcare, housing, healthcare)
- State benefits rules (TANF, SNAP, housing, childcare phase-outs)
- Job market data (in-demand roles, wage trends, employer hiring)
- Wage record data (for outcome validation, optional but preferred)

**To Evaluate:**
- Baseline employment & earnings (pre-job-loss)
- Intervention service utilization (what services accessed, when, duration)
- Participant outcomes (reemployment status, wage, benefits status at 30-day, 90-day, 1-year)
- Comparison group outcomes (matched cohort receiving standard services)

## Procurement Path

**To Procure Framework 02, institution must:**

1. **Secure Budget Authority** (1-2 months)
   - Identify funding source (UI fund, WIOA, state discretionary)
   - Develop business case for leadership approval

2. **Establish Governance** (1 month)
   - Designate program sponsor (state DOL, workforce board)
   - Convene steering committee (DOL, workforce, emergency assistance)
   - Draft interagency MOU (UI, WIOA, TANF, emergency assistance)

3. **Issue RFP for Lead Partner** (2 months)
   - Select implementation organization (nonprofit, contractor, or state agency)
   - Negotiate contract terms (performance metrics, pricing)
   - Establish timeline for staff hiring & onboarding

4. **Build Systems & Train** (3 months)
   - Procure/build assessment platform
   - Develop state-specific benefits calculator
   - Train workforce staff on benefits navigation
   - Establish data security protocols

5. **Launch Pilot** (Month 6)
   - Begin participant recruitment
   - Deliver services to 50-75 worker pilot
   - Track outcomes at 30-day, 90-day, 6-month, 1-year

**Total Timeline to Pilot Launch:** 6 months  
**Cost:** $100-150K for planning/setup + $400K for 12-month pilot (50-75 workers)

## ROI Logic

**Cost per Participant (30-day intervention):** $1,178

**Benefits per Participant (3-year horizon):**
- Accelerated reemployment (6 weeks faster): $5,400
- Improved wage trajectory (8% increase): $11,232
- Avoided financial instability (housing, debt, emergency assistance): $1,750
- Government cost savings (UI/emergency assistance reduction): $4,400
- **Total benefit:** $22,782

**ROI (Modeled, not validated):** 18.3x (in conservative scenarios, 4.8x)  
*Note: These are assumptions-based ROI scenarios. Actual ROI depends on pilot outcomes.*

**Payback Period (Assumptions-Based):** Modeled scenario shows benefits exceed costs within ~1 week of reemployment  
*Note: Actual payback depends on intervention effectiveness, labor market, participant characteristics.*

**Break-Even Threshold (Assumptions-Based):** Model shows break-even at 0.2 weeks of accelerated employment OR 1% wage improvement  
*Note: Pilot will test if these thresholds are realistic.*

## Pilot Design

**Duration:** 12 months (6 months enrollment + 6 months tracking)

**Sample:** 
- 50-75 intervention participants (receive Framework 02)
- 50-75 comparison participants (receive standard services)
- Matched by demographics, industry, wage history

**Pilot Targets (Not Proven Outcomes — Goals for Testing):**
- 30-day reemployment rate (pilot target: ≥50%)
- Time to employment (pilot target: 12 weeks avg, improvement from 18 weeks baseline)
- Starting wage (pilot target: ≥95% of pre-loss wage)
- Program cost (pilot target: ≤$1,500 per participant)

*Note: These are targets for pilot testing, not claimed outcomes. Actual results will depend on implementation quality, labor market conditions, and participant characteristics.*

**Success Criteria (Scale Decision, Pilot Targets Not Proven):**

- ✓ If all pilot targets achieved → recommend full implementation testing
- ✓ If ROI positive → justify as workforce investment
- ✗ If outcomes fall significantly short → recommend redesign or discontinuation

## Current Evidence Status

### What We Know ✓
- Job displacement causes significant earnings loss (peer-reviewed research)
- Financial fragility is widespread (Federal Reserve data)
- Benefits cliffs create work disincentives (known to UI & TANF administrators)
- Early workforce intervention improves outcomes (WIOA evaluation studies)

### What We Don't Know Yet ⚠️
- Does 30-day specific intervention work better than standard services?
- What is the actual reemployment acceleration?
- Does benefits cliff navigation change acceptance decisions?
- What is the cost per participant in practice?

### Research Needed (Pilot Will Answer)
- 30-day outcomes comparison (intervention vs. comparison)
- Cost tracking (actual hours, platform costs)
- Outcome measurement (employment, wage, financial stability)
- Process evaluation (what works, what doesn't, barriers)

## Claims NOT Yet Ready for Public Use

**Avoid Claiming:**
- "Guaranteed 50% reemployment rate" ← Needs pilot validation
- "30-day intervention reduces 90-day recidivism by X%" ← Unproven
- "Cost-per-placement is 60% lower than traditional support" ← Unvalidated
- "Eliminates job-loss financial instability" ← Oversold

**Do Claim (Supported by Literature):**
- "Structured intervention during critical 30-day window can accelerate reemployment and reduce financial stress"
- "Framework coordinates existing systems (UI, workforce, emergency assistance) for faster, better results"
- "Early evidence from similar programs shows 15-25% faster reemployment and improved long-term outcomes"
- "Designed to test whether better coordination saves money and improves worker stability"
