# Framework 02: Pilot Guide

**Framework:** Job-Loss & Income-Shock Stabilizer  
**Pilot Duration:** 6 months (enrollment) + 6 months (tracking) = 12 months total  
**Target Cohort:** 50-75 displaced workers  
**Status:** Ready for Implementation

---

## Pilot Objectives

### Primary Objective
Test whether a 30-day structured intervention can accelerate reemployment and reduce financial instability following job loss.

### Secondary Objectives
1. Validate cost model ($1,200/worker)
2. Test benefits cliff navigation approach
3. Assess staff capacity and training needs
4. Identify barriers to adoption and engagement
5. Establish comparison group for ROI validation

---

## Pilot Design

### Study Type
**Quasi-experimental with comparison group**

**Design:**
- Intervention cohort: 50-75 workers receiving Framework 02
- Comparison cohort: 50-75 matched workers (same demographics, industry, UI claims in same period)
- Comparison receives standard services (baseline UI, WIOA if available)
- Follow-up: 30-day, 90-day, 6-month, 12-month assessments

### Inclusion Criteria (Intervention Group)

**Must Include:**
- [ ] Job loss within past 14 days (new UI claimant or notice of layoff)
- [ ] Household income: $30K-150K annual (middle-income focus to test benefits cliff navigation)
- [ ] Age 18-65
- [ ] Able to engage in remote/in-person services
- [ ] English-literate (or access to interpreter)

**Can Include:**
- [ ] Any industry (construction, manufacturing, services, white-collar)
- [ ] Any education level
- [ ] Workers with disabilities (accommodations available)
- [ ] Prior workforce program participation

**Should Exclude:**
- [ ] Job loss resulting from termination for cause (different intervention needed)
- [ ] Workers with active criminal justice system involvement (different barriers)
- [ ] Workers with untreated mental health crises (safety first)

### Comparison Group Selection

**Matching Criteria:**
- [ ] Same geographic area
- [ ] Same industry or similar (within 1-2 digit NAICS)
- [ ] Similar wage history ($25K-175K range)
- [ ] Same gender
- [ ] Age within 5 years
- [ ] Job loss in same 2-week window

**Data Source:** State UI administrative data (use to identify matched cohort)

**Consent:** Comparison group receives standard services; data collected with consent for evaluation purposes only

## Pilot Workflow (30-Day Intensive Period)

### Day 1-3: Intake & Assessment

**Contact:** Reach out within 48 hours of UI claim

**Activities:**
- [ ] Initial conversation: 30 min (phone/video)
  - [ ] Confirm eligibility
  - [ ] Explain program (30 days, free)
  - [ ] Get consent for data sharing (UI records, optional benefits data)
  - [ ] Address immediate crisis if present (housing, food, utilities)
  
- [ ] Financial situation mapping: 60 min (video or in-person)
  - [ ] Income & expenses (current vs. pre-job-loss)
  - [ ] Savings & emergency funds
  - [ ] Debt (credit cards, auto, student, payday loans)
  - [ ] Housing stability
  - [ ] Benefits currently receiving or eligible for
  - [ ] Tools: Digital form or paper form (both options available)

- [ ] Career assessment: 45 min (phone or video)
  - [ ] Job history and skills
  - [ ] Target industry/role or open to exploration
  - [ ] Geographic flexibility
  - [ ] Education/credentials
  - [ ] Tools: Standard career assessment or simple questionnaire

**Output:**
- [ ] Completed intake profile in tracking system
- [ ] Initial financial situation summary
- [ ] Career exploration summary
- [ ] Personalized 30-day action plan drafted

**Participant Document Provided:**
- [ ] Program overview & expectations
- [ ] Calendared schedule of 4 core check-ins + office hours
- [ ] Contact information for case manager

---

### Days 4-7: Benefits & Financial Triage

**Objective:** Map out income sources & identify financial stabilization options within first week

**Activities:**

**1. Benefits Calculator Session: 60-90 min**
- [ ] Review current benefits (UI, SNAP, childcare assistance, housing help, LIHEAP)
- [ ] Map reemployment scenarios:
  - [ ] Scenario A: Return to similar-wage job at week 6
  - [ ] Scenario B: Bridge job (temporary, lower wage) + longer-term search
  - [ ] Scenario C: Part-time work while searching
  - [ ] Scenario D: Return to school/training (if relevant)
- [ ] For each scenario: Calculate take-home income & benefits cliff points
- [ ] Identify "danger zone" (where earned income drops benefits dramatically)
- [ ] Create personalized reemployment strategy accounting for cliffs
- [ ] Tools: State benefits calculator + case manager guidance + written summary

**2. Financial Triage: 45-60 min**
- [ ] Debt prioritization (which bills to pay first with limited cash)
- [ ] Emergency assistance eligibility check:
  - [ ] Rental assistance (if behind or at risk)
  - [ ] Utility assistance
  - [ ] Food assistance (SNAP emergency benefits)
  - [ ] Emergency grants (nonprofit organizations, churches, government)
- [ ] Housing stability planning:
  - [ ] Can household maintain housing on current resources?
  - [ ] If at risk: emergency assistance applications
  - [ ] Landlord communication templates/support
- [ ] Insurance review (maintain health insurance? COBRA vs. ACA options?)
- [ ] Paycheck allocation plan (if interim income needed)

**3. Financial Resources Toolkit: Provided in writing**
- [ ] State UI calculator link
- [ ] Benefits eligibility quiz
- [ ] Emergency assistance by state
- [ ] Credit counseling resources (free services)
- [ ] Gig work options (if interim income strategy)
- [ ] Childcare assistance contacts
- [ ] 211.org information

**Participant Documents Provided:**
- [ ] Benefits scenario summary (2-page: "Your Financial Situation After Job Loss")
- [ ] Reemployment strategy memo ("What to Expect at Different Wage Levels")
- [ ] Financial triage action plan (7-10 items prioritized)
- [ ] Emergency contacts & resources

**Staff Coordination Note:**
- [ ] Career advisor begins job search support in parallel
- [ ] Case manager monitors for crisis (eviction, utilities, food insecurity)

---

### Days 8-21: Job Search Acceleration

**Objective:** Structure job search within identified benefit-aware wage/timeline targets

**Activities:**

**1. Job Search Bootcamp (Introduction): 90 min, Days 8-9**
- [ ] Current job market overview for participant's field/region
- [ ] Job search strategy:
  - [ ] Target employers (10-15 specific companies identified)
  - [ ] Target roles (top 3-5 priority roles, wage alignment)
  - [ ] Search strategy (LinkedIn, industry boards, recruiters, networking)
  - [ ] Timeline targets (apply to X companies by day 21)
- [ ] Tools provided:
  - [ ] Employer target list (customized per person)
  - [ ] Networking script & contacts (alumni, industry associations)
  - [ ] Job board login credentials (CareerOneStop, LinkedIn, Indeed premium trial)

**2. Resume & Profile Optimization: 90 min, Days 10-12**
- [ ] Resume review & refresh:
  - [ ] Updated to current standards
  - [ ] Keyword optimization (for ATS systems)
  - [ ] Achievement-focused (impact, not just responsibilities)
  - [ ] 2-page max, clean format
- [ ] LinkedIn profile setup/optimization:
  - [ ] Professional photo, headline, summary
  - [ ] Skills endorsements, recommendations
  - [ ] Open to work status
  - [ ] LinkedIn network activation
- [ ] Cover letter templates:
  - [ ] 2-3 templates for different role types
  - [ ] Customization guidance for specific applications
- [ ] Deliverable: Participant leaves with updated resume/LinkedIn, ready to apply

**3. Interview Coaching: 60 min, Days 13-16**
- [ ] Mock interview (video recorded for playback)
  - [ ] Common questions + answers
  - [ ] Questions for interviewer
  - [ ] Managing job loss conversation ("why did you leave?")
  - [ ] Handling employment gaps
- [ ] Technical interview prep (if relevant to role)
- [ ] Salary negotiation basics
- [ ] Post-offer considerations (benefits, cliffs, relocation)
- [ ] Deliverable: Participant confident in interview process

**4. Network Activation: Ongoing throughout Days 8-21**
- [ ] Identify 10-15 professional contacts (former colleagues, mentors, alumni)
- [ ] Draft networking email template (authentic, not generic)
- [ ] Coffee chat talking points (what to ask, how to present)
- [ ] Follow-up strategy (LinkedIn connection, email, phone)
- [ ] Tracking: Log outreach and responses

**5. Accountability: Weekly Check-ins**
- [ ] Touchpoints: Days 8, 15, 21 (30 min each)
  - [ ] How many applications completed?
  - [ ] Any interview requests?
  - [ ] Barriers encountered?
  - [ ] Adjustments needed?
  - [ ] Financial/emergency needs addressed?

**Participant Documents Provided:**
- [ ] Job search action plan ("30-Day Reemployment Timeline")
- [ ] Employer target list (customized)
- [ ] Updated resume & cover letter templates
- [ ] Interview coaching notes
- [ ] LinkedIn optimization checklist
- [ ] Networking contact template

---

### Days 22-30: Interview Support & Decision-Making

**Objective:** Maximize job offers & support informed acceptance decisions

**Activities:**

**1. Offer Evaluation & Negotiation: As offers come in**
- [ ] Offer review (role, wage, benefits, location):
  - [ ] How does wage align with benefits cliff strategy?
  - [ ] Is offer above or below cliff threshold (use calculator)?
  - [ ] Benefits comparison (health insurance, retirement, flexibility)?
  - [ ] Timing of offer (start date, training period)?
- [ ] Negotiation guidance:
  - [ ] Wage negotiation talking points (if underbid)
  - [ ] Benefits negotiation (if sparse)
  - [ ] Flextime/remote options
  - [ ] Training time/ramp-up period
- [ ] Cliffware decision support:
  - [ ] If wage puts household above cliff threshold: What's the true take-home?
  - [ ] If wage is below cliff: Are there temporary assistance options (childcare subsidy continuation)?
  - [ ] Second income strategies (partner work, gig income)?
- [ ] Deliverable: Informed acceptance decision aligned with financial plan

**2. Final Check-in (Days 28-30): 60 min**
- [ ] Program close-out:
  - [ ] Participant acceptance decision (employed, still searching, other)?
  - [ ] If employed: Start date, role, wage confirmation
  - [ ] If still searching: Extended support options (WIOA, community resources)
  - [ ] Financial situation update (bridge income needed? Still on track?)
- [ ] 30-day survey:
  - [ ] Program satisfaction
  - [ ] What was most helpful?
  - [ ] What would you change?
  - [ ] Confidence level in new role / financial stability?
- [ ] Outcomes recorded:
  - [ ] Employment status (employed, still searching, other)
  - [ ] Wage (starting wage if employed)
  - [ ] Benefits taken (UI ending date if employed)
  - [ ] Financial stability indicators

**Participant Documents Provided:**
- [ ] Offer evaluation template
- [ ] Benefits/cliffs summary for accepted offer
- [ ] Orientation checklist (things to handle first day on job)
- [ ] Extended support options (if not yet employed)
- [ ] Program feedback survey

## Tracking & Outcomes Measurement

### 30-Day Outcomes (End of Intensive Period)

**Primary Outcomes:**
- [ ] Reemployment status (employed by day 30, % of cohort)
- [ ] Time to employment (average days to job acceptance)
- [ ] Starting wage (avg, min, max, by industry)
- [ ] Benefits utilization (% utilizing each resource: benefits calculator, financial triage, job search support)

**Secondary Outcomes:**
- [ ] Program engagement (% completing full intake, benefits session, interview prep)
- [ ] Participant satisfaction (4+ on 5-point scale)
- [ ] Financial stability indicators (housing secure, utilities paid, emergency fund status)

**Process Outcomes:**
- [ ] Participant attendance rate (% completing core sessions)
- [ ] Staff time tracking (hours spent per participant, by service type)
- [ ] Cost per participant (actual vs. budgeted $1,200)

### 90-Day Outcomes (Follow-up)

**Employment Outcomes:**
- [ ] Still employed (vs. quit, laid off again, withdrew)
- [ ] Wage trajectory (same, increased, decreased)
- [ ] Role fit (in target field? Desired industry?)

**Financial Outcomes:**
- [ ] Housing stability maintained
- [ ] Debt changes (reduced, stable, increased)
- [ ] Emergency fund (rebuilding?)
- [ ] Benefits status (UI ended? SNAP still active? Childcare subsidy?)

**Satisfaction/Experience:**
- [ ] Job satisfaction (3+ on 5-point scale)
- [ ] Would you recommend this program? (yes/no)

### 6-Month & 12-Month Outcomes

**Long-term Employment:**
- [ ] Employment status (employed, searching, other)
- [ ] Wage (current, change from day 30 placement)
- [ ] Career progression (same role, promoted, role change)
- [ ] Retention (% still with same employer)

**Long-term Financial:**
- [ ] Financial stress/resilience (survey)
- [ ] Savings rebuilt
- [ ] Housing stability
- [ ] Overall household income (participant + household members)

**Comparison Group Outcomes:**
- [ ] Same outcomes tracked for comparison cohort
- [ ] Difference in outcomes attributed to program intervention

## Data Collection & Privacy

### Data Elements to Collect

**At Intake:**
- Demographics (age, gender, education, location)
- Job history & wage
- Household composition & income
- Benefits receipt (UI, SNAP, childcare, housing)
- Financial situation snapshot

**During 30 Days:**
- Service utilization (what services accessed, when, duration)
- Staff notes (key barriers, progress notes, adjustments)
- Application activity (# applications, interviews received)
- Financial situation updates (emergency assistance accessed?)
- Engagement (attended sessions? completed activities?)

**At 30 Days & Follow-ups:**
- Employment status & offer details
- Starting wage & benefits
- Financial stability assessment
- Program satisfaction
- Outcomes survey

### Data Privacy & Security

- [ ] Participant consent form:
  - [ ] Explain data uses (program evaluation, outcome tracking)
  - [ ] Explain data sharing (UI data, wage records for outcome verification)
  - [ ] Explain retention (how long data kept)
  - [ ] Explain confidentiality (who can see their data)
  
- [ ] Data security:
  - [ ] Encrypted storage (cloud or secure server)
  - [ ] Limited staff access (case managers, evaluator only)
  - [ ] De-identified for reporting (remove names, use ID numbers)
  - [ ] Participant access (can request their own data)

- [ ] Evaluation confidentiality:
  - [ ] External evaluator (independent analysis)
  - [ ] Results reported in aggregate (no individual identification)
  - [ ] Participant consent for wage record verification (for outcome validation)

## Recruitment Strategy

### Where to Find Participants

**Primary Sources:**
1. **State UI System:** Direct outreach to new claimants in target demographics
2. **Employer Layoffs:** Partner with large employers undergoing reductions
3. **Workforce Development Boards:** Referrals from existing dislocated worker programs
4. **Community Partners:** Nonprofits, church job ministries, community centers

### Outreach Message

**Key Points:**
- Free 30-day support following job loss
- Help with benefits, job search, financial planning
- No obligation to participate (compare to control group)
- Can start immediately after job loss

### Enrollment Target
- **Goal:** 50-75 intervention + 50-75 comparison = 100-150 total
- **Timeline:** 8-12 week enrollment period
- **Success rate:** Expect 40-50% of outreached to enroll (so reach 200-300 to hit goal)

## Success Criteria for Scale Decision (Month 12)

### Go/No-Go Metrics

**Must Achieve (Minimum):**
- [ ] 30-day reemployment rate: ≥50% employed or has job offer
- [ ] Cost per participant: ≤$1,500 (slightly above model to account for pilot inefficiency)
- [ ] Program satisfaction: ≥4.0 on 5-point scale
- [ ] Participant retention: ≥70% completed full 30-day intervention

**Positive Indicators (Desirable but not required):**
- [ ] 90-day employment rate: ≥60% still employed
- [ ] Starting wage: ≥95% of pre-job-loss wage
- [ ] Comparison group showed lower employment rates (attribution to program)
- [ ] ROI positive (benefits exceed costs)

### Go Decision Criteria

**If all "Must Achieve" met:**
- Recommend full implementation
- Plan for scale-up to 300-400 workers/year

**If some "Must Achieve" not met but results promising:**
- Recommend pilot year 2 with modifications
- Address barriers identified in year 1

**If majority of "Must Achieve" not met:**
- Recommend program redesign or discontinuation
- Document lessons learned for field
