# Framework 02: Research Notes

**Framework:** Job-Loss & Income-Shock Stabilizer  
**Research Phase:** Literature review and source mapping  
**Status:** In progress - Level 2 evidence foundation

---

## Core Hypothesis

**Statement:** A structured 30-day stabilization intervention during job loss can prevent cascading financial instability and accelerate return to work, particularly by addressing benefits cliffs and information gaps.

**Why this matters:** Job loss is an acute financial and psychological shock. Without structured intervention in the first 30 days, households make reactive decisions (accepting any income regardless of career trajectory, taking on high-interest debt, delaying bill payments) that compound harm over the next 3 years.

---

## Research Landscape Summary

### 1. Job Displacement and Earnings Recovery

**Key Finding:** Job loss causes permanent earnings losses, not temporary dips.

**Literature:**
- Jacobson, LaLonde, Sullivan (1993): Workers displaced from manufacturing face 20-40% long-term earnings loss
- Couch & Placzek (2010): Even 5+ years after displacement, earnings haven't fully recovered
- Davis & von Wachter (2011): Displacement effects on mortality, health, family structure

**Implication for Framework:** The 30-day window is critical because it determines whether displaced workers:
- Retrain for higher-wage sectors (takes 6-12 months)
- Accept lower-wage work (permanent trajectory impact)
- Exit workforce (health, caregiving, discouraged)

**Source Status:** ✓ Available in academic literature, needs citation compilation

---

### 2. Financial Fragility Following Income Shock

**Key Finding:** Most U.S. households lack emergency reserves; income loss triggers debt/housing instability.

**Literature:**
- Federal Reserve SHED: ~40% of adults couldn't cover $400 emergency (as of 2023)
- Bricker et al. (Federal Reserve Board): Household financial fragility analysis
- Lusardi, Schneider, Tufano: Financial literacy and emergency preparedness

**Data Available:**
- BLS Employment Cost Survey: Wage replacement rates by industry
- DOL WARN Act Database: Large layoff tracking
- State UI administrative data: Claim volumes and replacement rates by state

**Implication for Framework:** Job loss is not just earnings loss—it's a compound crisis (income → housing → healthcare → family stability). The framework must address all four simultaneously.

**Source Status:** ✓ Federal Reserve data publicly available, state UI data available by request

---

### 3. Benefits Cliffs and Work Disincentives

**Key Finding:** Means-tested benefits phase out at specific income thresholds, creating effective marginal tax rates exceeding 100%.

**Literature:**
- Institute for Research on Poverty: TANF, SNAP, Housing Assistance cliff analysis
- Center for Economic and Policy Research: Effective tax rates for low-income workers
- Tax Foundation: State-by-state benefit cliff impacts

**State Examples:**
- Many states: Losing $1 of SNAP for every $1 of earnings (100% implicit tax)
- Housing assistance: Phase-out over 30% of income (30-60% effective tax when combined)
- Childcare subsidies: Cliff at specific income thresholds

**Implication for Framework:** The 30-day window includes benefits recalculation. Reemployment strategy must account for benefit cliffs—sometimes part-time work or delayed full-time start is more rational than appearing rational.

**Source Status:** ✓ State benefit manuals document cliffs; academic analysis available

---

### 4. Unemployment Insurance Adequacy

**Key Finding:** UI replacement rates vary by state (typically 40-50% of prior wage) and duration (26 weeks typical, varies by state and unemployment rate).

**Literature:**
- Department of Labor: UI program statistics by state
- Schmieder, von Wachter, Bender: Extended UI effects on employment and earnings
- National Academy of Sciences: UI program evaluation

**Current Data:**
- Average UI weekly benefit: ~$385 (varies $200-$900 by state)
- Typical duration: 26 weeks (6 months)
- Total UI benefit: ~$10,000 for average worker

**Implication for Framework:** UI alone cannot sustain a household. The 30-day window is when alternative income sources must be identified (partner income, savings, gig work, reduced expenses, or emergency assistance).

**Source Status:** ✓ DOL data publicly available

---

### 5. Early Intervention in Workforce Programs

**Key Finding:** Early engagement with workforce support (within days of job loss, not weeks/months) improves outcomes.

**Literature:**
- Jacobson & Rosenbloom: Rapid reemployment services
- WIOA evaluation studies: State-by-state program evaluations available
- Supported employment research: Early, intensive support works

**Program Examples:**
- Georgia CONNECT Program: Rapid response to major layoffs
- "Work-Share" programs (CA, NY): Employer/employee cost-sharing during reduction
- Incumbent worker training: Proactive upskilling before displacement

**Implication for Framework:** The framework should trigger within 3-7 days of job loss notice, not after benefits application.

**Source Status:** ✓ Program evaluations available; WIOA data accessible through state workforce boards

---

### 6. Employer Transition Support Effectiveness

**Key Finding:** Employer-sponsored outplacement and transition support improves outcomes.

**Literature:**
- SHRM: Employer-provided transition support prevalence and outcomes
- EAP industry data: Employee Assistance Program utilization and effectiveness
- Severance/outplacement ROI studies

**Data Available:**
- ADP Research Institute: Employer transition practices
- Paychex/NFIB: Small business transition support
- Staffing industry reports on placement rates

**Implication for Framework:** Coordination between employers (during transition) and post-employment support systems (UI, workforce boards) is rare but high-impact.

**Source Status:** ⚠️ Fragmented; requires primary research through SHRM and ADP

## Evidence Gaps Requiring Research

### Gap 1: 30-Day Specific Evidence
**Problem:** Literature covers "early intervention" generically, not specifically 30-day timing  
**Why it matters:** Policy/program design depends on interval length  
**Research needed:** Retrospective analysis of job-loss timelines in existing WIOA, TAA, Trade Adjustment Assistance data

### Gap 2: Outcomes in Current Programs
**Problem:** Program evaluations exist but don't isolate 30-day intervention effects  
**Why it matters:** Need to know: Does intervention within 30 days vs. day 60 change outcomes?  
**Research needed:** Reanalysis of existing program data with 30-day cohort isolation

### Gap 3: Technology-Enabled Intervention Cost
**Problem:** Most evidence is on human-intensive programs  
**Why it matters:** Framework assumes some tech-enabled guidance, not pure case management  
**Research needed:** Pilot with digital assessment + targeted case management

### Gap 4: Benefits Cliff Navigation Outcomes
**Problem:** Known that cliffs exist, but outcomes of navigation support are undocumented  
**Why it matters:** Are people successfully using cliff-aware work/benefit strategies?  
**Research needed:** Pilot data collection on reemployment after benefits guidance
