## Sourced Claims (Verified or In-Review)

### Claim: "The first 30 days of job loss determine the next 3 years of financial stability"

**Status:** Source identified, needs verification

**Relevant Sources:**
- U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics - Job Displacement and Earnings Loss
  - Tracks long-term earnings impacts following job loss
  - Source: https://www.bls.gov/news.release/disp.nr0.htm
  - Status: Available for review

- Department of Labor - Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) Outcomes
  - Measures employment outcomes and wage replacement
  - Status: Available through state workforce agencies

- Household Financial Fragility Research
  - Academic literature on financial resilience following income shocks
  - Researchers: Bricker, Kennickell, Moore, Sabelhaus (Federal Reserve Board)
  - Status: Peer-reviewed, needs full citation integration

**Citation Strategy:**
- Lead with BLS data on earnings recovery curves
- Support with WIOA outcomes from 3-5 state programs
- Include household financial fragility literature

**Confidence Level:** Level 2 (secondary research supported)

### Claim: "Without intervention, job loss frequently triggers cascading financial instability"

**Status:** Source identified, needs verification

**Relevant Sources:**
- National Bureau of Economic Research - Job Displacement Effects
  - Quantifies spillover effects on housing stability, healthcare, family structure
  - Status: Peer-reviewed literature available

- Federal Reserve - Survey of Household Economics and Decisionmaking (SHED)
  - Annual survey on financial resilience and emergency preparedness
  - Source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/consumerscommunities/shed.htm
  - Status: Accessible

- Annie E. Casey Foundation - Economic Security Program
  - Research on income volatility and family stability
  - Status: Research reports available

**Citation Strategy:**
- Use SHED data to establish baseline financial vulnerability
- Cite NBER studies on downstream effects
- Include nonprofit research on emergency assistance demand

**Confidence Level:** Level 2 (secondary research supported)

### Claim: "Benefits cliffs trap people in poverty and discourage return to work"

**Status:** Source identified, needs verification

**Relevant Sources:**
- Council of Economic Advisers - Benefits Cliff Documentation
  - Analysis of marginal tax rates including benefit phase-outs
  - Status: Government white papers available

- Institute for Research on Poverty - TANF and SNAP Benefit Cliffs
  - Research on how benefits phase out above income thresholds
  - Status: Peer-reviewed research available

- State Department of Social Services - Benefit Calculation Examples
  - Specific examples showing income cliff impacts
  - Status: Available from all 50 state systems

**Citation Strategy:**
- Use state benefit calculators for concrete examples
- Cite IRP research on disincentive effects
- Include CEA analysis of marginal effective tax rates

**Confidence Level:** Level 2 (secondary research supported)
